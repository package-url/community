## Notes

- Meeting minutes are being kept and will be published, and the meeting will be recorded with Google Meet video and Gemini "note-taking".
- Our code of conduct (link in agenda above) applies to this meeting.
- Introductions.
- Summary
  - Participants integrated recent pull requests and evaluated third-party tools to improve project communications while avoiding self-hosting administrative burdens. They agreed to finalize documentation as non-normative appendices.
  - The team aligned on strict parsing requirements and adopted the Package-URL model for handling private namespaces and URL quoting. They established that advanced tests remain secondary to base validation.
  - Governance for private namespace registration will mirror established models to prevent collisions. Members plan to support implementer adoption through best practices and broader industry outreach efforts.
- **Roll Call and Initial Updates**: Michael Herzog and Philippe Ombredanne began the meeting with a roll call, followed by an introduction from Jonatan Männchen regarding ongoing research into VERS. Immanuel Kunz provided an update on the recent merger of a canonical pull request for the VERS specification and noted that a follow-up issue is needed to provide guidelines for flexible parsing to ensure implementers have a path for recovery without repeating previous mistakes made with Package-URL.
- **Administrative and Specification Issues**: Immanuel Kunz raised two additional items: Issue 52 concerning URL quoting, which is considered a simple task, and Issue 17 regarding reserved private namespaces. Steve Springett joined the meeting and expressed agreement with the agenda items.
- **Proposal for Official Outreach**: Michael Herzog introduced Community Issue 27, which explores options for formalizing outreach to notify interested parties about major project changes. They proposed using either a hosted mailing list service like groups.io or an open-source tool like Listmonk to manage outbound communications while redirecting actual discussions to GitHub or Slack.
- **Regulatory Compliance and Outreach Strategy**: Steve Springett cautioned against self-hosting a mailing list due to the administrative burden of General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) compliance. The group expressed a preference for an established third-party provider or service to handle these communications while maintaining a focus on directing feedback to GitHub issues to avoid creating parallel, fragmented discussion threads.
- **Project Health and Outreach Tools**: Michael Herzog mentioned interest in Listmonk, noting its popularity and focus on outbound newsletter management. Philippe Ombredanne added that the project is well-regarded and managed by a team associated with an Indian financial institution. Michael Herzog committed to further investigating hosting options to ensure the project remains sustainable.
- **VERS Renaming and Test Schema Updates**: Michael Herzog confirmed the merger of the proposal to rename VERS components, resulting in the creation of `vers-test.schema-0.2.json`. Johannes Pein has provided detailed feedback regarding potential errors in the new schema, which the team plans to review and address (https://github.com/package-url/vers-spec/issues/68) (see also https://github.com/package-url/vers-spec/pull/67).
- **VERS Background Research**: Jonatan Männchen presented preliminary work on VERS versioning, categorizing use cases into retrospective and prospective scenarios. They emphasized that this is currently background information rather than a formal specification proposal, and they intend to refine this material over the coming weeks with potential feedback from a mathematician regarding set theory.
- **Role of Non-Normative Appendices**: The group discussed the structure of the VERS documentation. They agreed that while Jonatan Männchen's detailed research is valuable, it should be treated as non-normative background information or included in an appendix to the specification, consistent with how they previously managed background context in the docs folder.
- **Vulnerability Database Conflict Resolution**: Philippe Ombredanne introduced a project aimed at resolving conflicting version ranges in vulnerability advisories. They noted that databases often source information from the same upstream providers but damage the data during processing. The team is developing an automated, decentralized user interface and automation logic to help humans reconcile these intersecting, non-overlapping version sets.
- **Best Practices for Adoption**: The participants discussed the need for best practices to assist with the adoption of VERS. Steve Springett suggested that even if VERS cannot cover 100% of all edge cases, providing best practices would be highly beneficial for tool implementers.
- **Strictness in Parsing**: The group deliberated on the necessity of "flexible" parsing. Philippe Ombredanne argued that they might not need to support recovery from weird, non-canonical formatting, favoring a strict approach that mandates canonical form to prevent downstream complications. They concluded that they do not need to make a final decision today but lean toward prioritizing strict adherence to the specification.
- **Purpose of Advanced Tests**: Michael Herzog and Philippe Ombredanne discussed the role of "advanced" tests. They clarified that these tests serve as useful documentation and helper utilities to identify errors in input, but they should not act as a substitute for "base" tests, which must be passed for a version to be considered valid.
- **Communication with Tool Implementers**: Michael Herzog expressed concern regarding the inconsistency in reaching out to open-source tool implementers. Jonatan Männchen offered to assist by leveraging their connections within the Linux Foundation to reach a broader list of package manager maintainers when necessary.
- **URL Quoting Specification**: Immanuel Kunz discussed Issue 52 and the need to align VERS' URL quoting with the Package-URL specification. Although Jonatan Männchen initially raised concerns about double encoding, the group concluded that because VERS is not a location indicator, the potential for double encoding does not present a significant issue.
- **AI-Assisted Mapping**: Philippe Ombredanne shared an anecdotal update regarding an external project using artificial intelligence, specifically Qwen and GPT, to map Common Platform Enumeration identifiers to Package-URL. They noted this is intended to help security teams transition away from legacy identification methods.
- **Private Namespaces and Registration**: Michael Herzog and Immanuel Kunz discussed the importance of managing registrations for private namespaces. Michael Herzog emphasized that any registration process must allow for due diligence to prevent future collisions while still enabling private, proprietary use cases.
- **Requirements for Private Schemes**: Immanuel Kunz summarized the requirements for private name spaces: they must prevent long-term collisions, maintain minimal specification overhead, and provide a clear upgrade path to registered status. Michael Herzog suggested that the Package-URL Registry project, funded by the European Union, serves as a suitable model for handling these requirements.
- **Qualifier Registration**: Michael Herzog noted that the process for registering qualifiers should be similar to namespace registration. While the system should be flexible enough to allow innovation, it should also support common qualifiers from ecosystems like RPM or Debian.
- The meeting was adjourned.
